About Me
I am a first-year Ph.D. student at University of California, Los Angeles, advised by Prof. Yuan Tian and work closely with Prof. Tony Nowatzki. I also collaborated closely with Prof. Niraj Jha from Princeton University and Prof. Christina Delimitrou from CSAIL at MIT. I have broad interests in secure computer systems and trustworthy machine learning. The way how hardware, software, and data interact with each other appeals to me the most. My recent research focuses on efficient management of emerging new workloads in heterogeneous datacenters and constructing systems for privacy-preserve machine learning. I formerly interned at NVIDIA, Alibaba, Shanghai AI Institute. My research is fully supported by Samueli Engineering Fellowship at UCLA.

Before joining UCLA, I obtained my Bachelor of Science in Computer Sicence and Bachelor of Engineering in Microelectronics from Shanghai Jiao Tong University. I graduated summa cum laude in Electrical Computer Engineering from SJTU. During my undergraduate, I was a visiting researcher in Computer Science at The University of Texas at Austin and also an exchange in Computer Science at The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology. I was a member of ACM and IEEE, and a member of Computer Architecture Student Association (CASA) and Students@Systems supported by ACM SIGARCH.
